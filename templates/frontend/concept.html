Project Concept

Introduction

KickOutHunger is a transformative university feeding program designed to support students from less fortunate backgrounds who struggle to afford daily meals at the campus cafeteria. Recognizing that proper nutrition is vital for academic success and overall well-being, Project Inuka provides a sustainable solution to ensure no student has to go hungry. By leveraging the power of community and the generosity of well-wishers, corporate partners, and alumni, Project Inuka aims to create a supportive environment where every student can thrive.

Alleviate Hunger

Ensure that all university students, regardless of their financial background, have access to at least one nutritious meal a day.

Support Academic Success

Promote better academic performance and overall well-being by addressing food insecurity among students.

Build a Supportive Community

Engage alumni, corporate partners, and well-wishers in a meaningful way to support the next generation of graduates.

Crowdsourcing for Funds

  • Website Platform: Project Inuka raises funds through the crowdsourcing website "project-inua comrade".
  • Donations: The platform accepts one-time gifts, monthly donations, and gifts in kind from well-wishers, corporate partners, and alumni.
  • Transparency: Regular updates and reports are provided to donors to maintain transparency and accountability.

Application for Meal Cards

  • Student Application: Students who face financial challenges and can barely afford meals can apply for a meal card through the Project Inuka website.
  • Vetting Process: Applications are vetted with the assistance of the campus administration to ensure that support reaches the students most in need.

Distribution of Meal Cards

  • Issuance of Cards: Qualified students receive a meal card loaded with credit that covers the cost of meals for a semester.
  • Accessibility: These meal cards can be used at the campus cafeteria, ensuring easy access to nutritious food.

Impact

Project Inuka is designed to make a tangible difference in the lives of university students by addressing food insecurity head-on. By ensuring that students have access to regular meals, the program helps improve their concentration, academic performance, and overall mental and physical health. Additionally, the initiative fosters a sense of community and shared responsibility among donors, creating a network of support that extends beyond the campus.

Well-Wishers and Alumni

Donate: Make a one-time gift or set up a monthly donation through the Project Inuka website.

Share: Spread the word about Project Inuka within your networks to help raise awareness and funds.

Corporate Partners

Partnership Opportunities: Engage in corporate social responsibility by partnering with Project Inuka to provide financial support, food donations, or other resources.

Employee Engagement: Encourage your employees to participate in fundraising activities or volunteer their time to support the program.
